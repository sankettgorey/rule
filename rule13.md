**Summary of Electric Rule No. 13 (Temporary Service) – PG&E Tariff Rules**  
*Effective February 28, 2024, with key changes effective January 1, 2025*  

---

### **Scope of the Document**  
This document outlines **PG&E’s rules for temporary electric service** under California Public Utility Commission (CPUC) regulations. It defines when temporary service is available, how customers are charged, and conditions for transitioning to permanent service. It also includes special provisions for disaster-affected customers and new construction projects.  

---

### **Key Provisions**  

#### **1. Temporary Service Establishment**  
- **Who Pays**: Applicants (customers) must pay **estimated costs** for installation and removal of temporary facilities, minus salvage value.  
- **Payment Terms**:  
  - **Advance Payment**: Fees are paid upfront or as required by PG&E.  
  - **Credit Requirements**: Applicants must meet credit rules (Rule 6), but the **deposit** cannot exceed the **estimated bill** for the service duration.  
- **Exceptions**:  
  - **Disaster Relief**: Customers affected by disasters (per CPUC’s Emergency Consumer Protection Plan) **waive fees**.  
  - **Camp Fire Impacted Customers**: Eligible customers can get **free underground service pedestals** (via Form 79-1204) until the program completes.  

#### **2. Applicant Design Option**  
- **Option**: Customers may design their own temporary facilities (instead of PG&E).  
- **Charges/Refunds**: All costs and refunds are governed by **Rule 13**, not other rules.  

#### **3. Transition to Permanent Service**  
- **When Temporary Status Ends**:  
  - If service is deemed **speculative**, **short-term**, or **questionable in permanency**, the customer remains temporary.  
  - If operations change to **permanent status**, PG&E refunds overpaid amounts.  
- **Refund Calculation**: Refunds are based on the **extension rule** in effect at the time of reclassification.  

#### **4. Changes for New Construction Projects (2025+)**
- **Mixed-Fuel New Construction**: Starting **January 1, 2025**, customers pay **actual costs** for Electric Line Extensions (instead of estimated costs) at project completion.

---

### **Who Pays and What They Pay For** 
| **Payer** | **What They Pay For** | When** |
|-------------------------|------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| **Temporary Service Applicants** | Estimated installation/removal costs, deposits (up to estimated bill)| When applying for temporary service (no undue hardship).                 |       
| **Disaster-Affected Customers** | No fees (waived under CPUC’s Emergency Consumer Protection Plan)                     | During or after qualifying disasters (e.g., wildfires, earthquakes).     |       
| **Camp Fire Impacted Customers** | No additional cost for underground pedestals (via Form 79-1204)                      | During the Pedestal Program’s active period.                             |      
| **New Construction Projects (2025+)** | Actual costs of Electric Line Extensions (not estimated)                             | At project completion for Mixed-Fuel New Construction.                   | 

---

### **Key Takeaways**
- **Temporary Service**: Available if PG&E can provide it without undue hardship. Costs are based on estimates, with deposits capped at the expected bill.
- **Refunds**: Overpaid amounts are refunded if temporary service transitions to permanent.
- **Special Cases**: Disaster victims and Camp Fire-impacted customers receive fee waivers or free infrastructure.
- **2025 Change**: New construction projects will pay actual costs for electric line extensions instead of estimates.

This document ensures transparency in temporary service costs, refunds, and exceptions, balancing customer needs with utility operational requirements.
