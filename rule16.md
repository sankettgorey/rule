The document outlines **PG&E's Rule 16**, which governs **Service Extensions** for electric service delivery. Below is a structured summary of key sections and their implications:

---

### **1. Definitions (Critical for Compliance)**
- **Applicant**: Any entity requesting electric service from PG&E.
- **Conduit**: Ducts, pipes, or tubes (metal, plastic, etc.) for protecting electrical wires, including pull wires and concrete encasement.
- **Distribution Lines**: Overhead/underground facilities operated at distribution voltages (per Rule 2), designed to supply multiple services.
- **Excavation**: Includes trenching, backfilling, surface repairs, and disposal of spoil for service installations.
- **Franchise Area**: Public rights-of-way where PG&E has legal access via franchise agreements.
- **Insignificant Loads**: Small, non-essential loads (e.g., gate openers, clocks).
- **Intermittent Loads**: Loads that may be discontinued temporarily.
- **Mixed-Fuel New Construction**: Buildings using gas/propane in addition to electricity, as defined by CEC 2022 standards.
- **Premises**: Real property and equipment for a single enterprise, excluding industrial/agricultural sites separated by public thoroughfares.
- **Protective Structures**: Fences, retaining walls, sound barriers, etc., required by PG&E.
- **Seasonal Service**: Service for seasonal or intermittent use (e.g., resorts, cottages).
- **Service Delivery Point**: Connection point between PG&E's facilities and the applicant's conductors.
- **Service Extensions**: Overhead/underground facilities from the Distribution Line to the Service Delivery Point.
- **Substructures**: Surface/subsurface structures supporting PG&E facilities (e.g., splice boxes, foundations).

---

### **2. Key Operational Rules**
#### **A. Existing Service Facilities**
- **Conversion to Underground**: 
  - If an overhead line is replaced underground (per Rule 20), new underground services are installed under Rule 16.
  - **Applicant's Responsibility**: Perform excavation, install substructures, and pay PG&E for service completion and overhead removal.
- **Damaged Facilities**: 
  - PG&E repairs damage caused by others at the responsible party's expense. Applicants repair their own facilities.
- **Subdivision of Premises**:
  - Subdividers must provide rights-of-way for PG&E. If not, PG&E may discontinue service without liability. Applicants pay relocation/removal costs.
- **Impaired Access/Clearances**:
  - Applicants must correct access/clearance issues or pay PG&E to relocate facilities. Failure may result in service discontinuation.

#### **B. Special Cases**
- **Exceptional Cases**: If Rule 16 application is impractical, PG&E or Applicants can refer to the **Commission** for special rulings or mutually agreed conditions.

---

### **3. Responsibilities and Costs**
- **Applicant Responsibilities**:
  - Pay for excavation, substructures, and service completion.
  - Repair their own facilities.
  - Provide rights-of-way during subdivisions.
- **PG&E Responsibilities**:
  - Install and maintain facilities (except for applicant-owned equipment).
  - Repair damage caused by third parties.
  - Discontinue service if rights-of-way are not provided.

---

### **4. Effective Dates and Compliance**
- **Effective Dates**: Rules became effective in **2024–2025**, impacting projects and compliance timelines.
- **Compliance**: Adherence to definitions and procedures is critical to avoid service disruptions or legal liabilities.

---

### **5. Practical Implications**
- **New Construction**: Mixed-fuel buildings must follow Rule 16 for service extensions.
- **Seasonal Use**: Service for seasonal loads (e.g., resorts) is permitted but may require specific provisions.
- **Conversion Projects**: Overhead-to-underground conversions require significant applicant involvement and cost.

---

### **Summary**
Rule 16 establishes a framework for **electric service extensions**, balancing PG&E's infrastructure responsibilities with Applicant obligations. Key takeaways include:
- **Clear definitions** for all terms to ensure compliance.
- **Defined responsibilities** for both parties (PG&E and Applicants).
- **Special provisions** for conversions, subdivisions, and exceptional cases.
- **Cost and procedural requirements** for installations, repairs, and service adjustments.

This rule ensures structured, compliant service delivery while addressing unique scenarios through exceptions and special rulings.
