**Summary of CPUC Rule 20: Underground Electric System Replacement**  

---

### **Overview**  
This document outlines **Rule 20** under California’s Public Utilities Commission (CPUC) regulations, which govern the replacement of **overhead electric facilities** with **underground systems**. The rule establishes conditions, responsibilities, and payment structures for utilities (specifically Pacific Gas and Electric Company, or PG&E) and applicants (cities, counties, or property owners) to transition to underground infrastructure.  

---

### **Key Provisions**  

#### **1. Rule 20A (City/County-Initiated Projects)**  
- **PG&E Covers Costs**:  
  - PG&E will **replace overhead lines** with underground systems **at their expense** if the project meets specific criteria.  
  - **Conditions**:  
    - The city/county must demonstrate **public interest** (e.g., heavy overhead lines, high-traffic areas, ADA compliance, scenic zones, or arterial streets).  
    - A **local ordinance** must mandate the removal of overhead facilities and the installation of underground service.  
    - **Minimum undergrounding**: At least **one block (600 feet)** of street must be converted.  

- **Work Credit Allocations**:  
  - PG&E allocates **work credits** (funding for underground projects) to cities/counties based on the number of overhead meters.  
  - **Expiry Dates**:  
    - Unallocated work credits expire by **June 8, 2025** (for Rule 20A).  
    - Credits not used for active projects expire by **June 8, 2025**.  
  - **No Trading**: Unauthorized work credit trading is prohibited, except for **intra-county transfers** or **pooling for community benefit**.  

- **Customer Compensation**:  
  - PG&E covers **up to $1,500 per service entrance** for converting electric panels and installing service laterals (up to 100 feet per customer).  
  - Cities/counties may set lower limits or restrict total spending per project.  

---

#### **2. Other Circumstances (Rule 20B)**
- **Applicant-Initiated Projects**:
  - **Property Owners Must Agree**: All property owners served by overhead lines must **write consent** to switch to underground service, or legislation must mandate the changes.
  - **Applicant Responsibilities**:
    - Install **pads, vaults, and infrastructure** (e.g., conduits, poles) per PG&E specs or pay PG&E to do so.
    - Transfer ownership of overhead facilities to PG&E.
    - Pay a **nonrefundable fee** equal to the cost of the underground system minus the salvage value of the overhead equipment.
  - **Minimum Coverage**: At least **one block (600 feet)** of street must be undergrounded, with all overhead facilities removed.

- **Engineering Costs**:
  - PG&E may fund initial **engineering/design costs** if requested by a city/county.
  - If the project is not approved within **2.5 years**, the city/county must reimburse PG&E for these costs.
  - Failure to reimburse reduces the city/county’s work credit allocations.

---

#### **3. Applicant-Initiated Projects (Rule 20C)**
- **Applicant Pays**:
  - The applicant (e.g., a private entity) must pay a **nonrefundable fee** for the underground system, minus the salvage value of the overhead equipment.
  - PG&E handles **installation and maintenance** of the underground system.

---

### **Who Pays and What They Pay For**

| **Scenario** | **Who Pays** | **What They Pay For** |                     
|---------------------------|----------------------| ---------------------------------------|
| **Rule 20A (City/County)** | PG&E                 | Removal of overhead lines, underground installation, customer service conversions     |
| **Rule 20B (Applicant)**  | City/County/Property Owners | Infrastructure (pads, vaults), ownership transfer, nonrefundable fee for underground system |
| **Rule 20C (Applicant)**  | Applicant            | Nonrefundable fee for underground system (minus overhead salvage value)               |

---

### **Situations Covered**
1. **Rule 20A**: City/county-led projects with public interest and local ordinance approval.
2. **Rule 20B**: Applicant-led projects requiring property owner consent or legislation.
3. **Rule 20C**: Private applicant-initiated projects where the applicant pays for the underground system.

---

### **Important Deadlines and Notes**
- **Work Credit Expiry**: Unallocated Rule 20A credits expire by **June 8, 2025**.
- **Unauthorized Trading**: Prohibited after **June 3, 2021**.
- **Engineering Costs**: Unreimbursed costs are charged to the city/county’s work credit allocations. 


---

**Summary**: Rule 20 provides a structured framework for transitioning overhead electric systems to underground infrastructure, with varying responsibilities and payment obligations based on the project’s origin (city/county, applicant, or private entity). PG&E covers most costs under Rule 20A, while applicants bear expenses in other scenarios. The rule emphasizes public interest, local governance, and financial accountability.
